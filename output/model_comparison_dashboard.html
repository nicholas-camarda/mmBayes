What this means

Doing well
  • Strongest round: Elite 8 with 83.3% accuracy and 0.169 Brier.
  • Closest calibration range: among 35 held-out games where the model gave one side about a 50% to 60% win probability, that side had an average predicted win rate of 55% and actually won 54% of the time. This does not mean those were the model's 'best predicted' games. It means that among games where the model gave one side about this win probability, that side's actual win rate landed closest to the stated probability.
Needs attention
  • Weakest round: First Four with 58.3% accuracy and 0.238 Brier.
  • Weakest calibration range: among 51 held-out games where the model gave one side about a 80% to 90% win probability, that side had an average predicted win rate of 85% but actually won 67% of the time. In this range the model was too optimistic by 18.6 percentage points. This does not mean those games were hardest overall. It means that among games where the model gave one side about this win probability, the actual win rate was furthest from the stated probability.

Backtest Calibration Curve

Observed win rate in that probability range
Perfect line

How to read this chart: each point groups held-out games into a probability range, such as 30% to 40%. Mean predicted is the model's average pregame win probability in that range, and empirical rate is how often that side actually won. So if a point sits near 35% on the x-axis, read it as: among games where the model gave one side about a 35% chance to win, how often did that side actually win? If the observed rate is also about 35%, that range is well calibrated. This is about long-run frequency matching, not whether those were the easiest or 'best' individual predictions. Points below the dashed line mean the model was too optimistic; points above it mean the model was too pessimistic.
