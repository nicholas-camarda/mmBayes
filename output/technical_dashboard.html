Interpret the dots and bars

Model Quality

What this shows

Backtest metrics for probability quality and pool-scoring quality, plus the calibration curve.

How to use it

Use this as a trust check. If calibration is poor or score metrics are weak, treat close dashboard recommendations with more skepticism.

Why it matters

Better probability calibration helps every downstream board. Bracket score matters most for pool decisions because it respects round weighting, not just raw pick count.

Underlying math

Log loss and Brier score reward accurate probabilities. Bracket score is the average pool points earned under standard round scoring, while correct picks is the average number of games chosen correctly.

The chart on the right is the calibration curve. The blue points are the observed win rates inside each predicted-probability bin. If that blue path stays near the dotted diagonal, the model's probabilities are lining up well with reality.

Bracket score means expected pool points under standard round scoring. It matters more than raw pick count because late-round misses cost much more.

Correct picks means the average number of games chosen correctly. It is intuitive, but it treats every game equally and is therefore less useful than bracket score for pool strategy.

Log loss and Brier score both measure probability quality. Lower is better because the model is assigning more honest probabilities.

Empirical rate means the observed win rate within each probability bin. Points above the diagonal indicate the model was under-confident in that region; points below it indicate over-confidence.
